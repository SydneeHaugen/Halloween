Fun Facts About Halloween

Full Moon on Halloween

Every 19 years, Halloween night gets a rare full moon, adding to the eerie atmosphere. According to NASA, it's a special event only seen about once every two decades, making it all the more magical when it happens!

Halloween’s Ancient Origins

Halloween traces back over 2,000 years to the Celtic festival of Samhain. Celebrated in early November, Samhain marked the end of the harvest and the thinning veil between the living and spirit worlds. People lit bonfires and wore costumes to ward off ghosts, a tradition rooted in ancient Ireland, the UK, and France.

Medieval Trick-or-Treating

The origins of trick-or-treating date back to medieval times as “guising” in Scotland and Ireland. Young people would go door-to-door in costumes, performing songs or poems in exchange for food or coins. Today, the tradition lives on with children collecting candy — though singing is optional!

Halloween Spending in America

Americans love Halloween, spending big on costumes, candy, and decorations. According to the National Retail Federation, people spend an average of $108 each, with total spending reaching billions on costumes, candy, and decorations alone.
